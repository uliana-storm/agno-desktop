# Last updated: May 2026

## Personality
Professional and established. Serious with soul. Authoritative, not
arrogant. Premium, not pretentious. Confident, not aggressive.
Think: senior broker at a private bank, not a startup founder.

## Four principles
1. **Dedicated broker, not platform** — reference humans, not systems.
   "Your broker will confirm this" not "The system will process this."
   Centre on the client: "You received" not "We sent."
2. **Confident authority** — state facts, no hedging. Never sound
   uncertain about our own capabilities.
3. **Make it effortless** — plain language, simple steps, anticipate
   the next question. Never make clients feel uninformed.
4. **Premium and considered** — use names, respect time. Every word
   intentional.

## Never use
- Emojis in product UI or email (social: max 1-2, professional only:
  📊 📈 💡 🔐 — never 🚀🌙💎🙌)
- Exclamation marks in product UI. Max 1 per page on website.
- Em dashes
- Semicolons in client-facing content
- ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Crypto slang: HODL, moon, whale, degen, WAGMI, sats, FUD, alpha, NFA
- Filler: just, simply, basically, actually, very, really
- Hype: amazing, incredible, revolutionary, game-changing
- Fake urgency: Act now, Don't miss out, Last chance
- Casual greetings: Hey, Hi there, Hey there

## Formatting
- Dates: DD-MMM-YYYY (e.g. 28-DEC-2025)
- Times: h:mm AM/PM TZ (e.g. 2:30 PM AEDT) — always include timezone
- Currency: amount + ISO code (e.g. 10,000 AUD, 0.5 BTC)
- Headlines: Title Case
- Buttons and labels: sentence case, no periods
- Body text: sentence case
- Oxford comma: always
- Contractions: allowed except in legal or compliance text
- Spelling: Australian English
- Website: spell out Bitcoin, Ethereum (not BTC, ETH)

## Email rules
- Subject lines: sentence case, no emoji, no clickbait, under 50 chars
- Greeting: first name if known, otherwise "Hello" or "Good morning"
- Sign-off: "The Stormrake Team" or "[Name], Your Stormrake Broker"
- Max 300-500 words for newsletters. One CTA per email.

## Primary audiences
**Busy professional** (35-55, high net worth, time-poor): get to the
point, no over-explaining, make actions obvious.

**Soon-to-retire investor** (55-70, protective of wealth, lower tech
comfort): more warmth, emphasise security and credentials, no jargon.

**Default assumption:** intelligent, busy, new to digital assets, wants
to trust us but hasn't yet.

**Never write for:** day traders, crypto enthusiasts, meme culture.
